𝗛𝗼𝗺𝗲-𝗺𝗮𝗱𝗲 𝗚𝗹𝗮𝘀𝘀 𝗟𝗟𝗠 · Post 2 of 6 · How AI chops up your text

Before any AI understands your text, it first chops it into small pieces. Most people never see this step, but 𝗶𝘁 𝗾𝘂𝗶𝗲𝘁𝗹𝘆 𝗰𝗼𝘀𝘁𝘀 𝘆𝗼𝘂 𝗺𝗼𝗻𝗲𝘆 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘀𝗽𝗮𝗰𝗲.

Here is why it matters. 𝗬𝗼𝘂 𝗽𝗮𝘆 𝗯𝘆 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗽𝗶𝗲𝗰𝗲, 𝗮𝗻𝗱 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗔𝗜 𝗰𝗮𝗻 𝗼𝗻𝗹𝘆 𝗵𝗼𝗹𝗱 𝘀𝗼 𝗺𝗮𝗻𝘆 𝗽𝗶𝗲𝗰𝗲𝘀 𝗮𝘁 𝗼𝗻𝗰𝗲. More pieces means a bigger bill and less room for your actual question.

I compared two versions of the same AI, built in exactly the same way. The only difference: one learned from medical writing, the other from everyday English. I gave them both the same medical sentence.

𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗺𝗲𝗱𝗶𝗰𝗮𝗹 𝗼𝗻𝗲 𝗯𝗿𝗼𝗸𝗲 𝗶𝘁 𝗶𝗻𝘁𝗼 𝟮𝟳 𝗽𝗶𝗲𝗰𝗲𝘀. 𝗧𝗵𝗲 𝗲𝘃𝗲𝗿𝘆𝗱𝗮𝘆 𝗼𝗻𝗲 𝗻𝗲𝗲𝗱𝗲𝗱 𝟰𝟯.

On a longer, real medical line the gap held: 𝟯𝟬 𝗽𝗶𝗲𝗰𝗲𝘀 𝗮𝗴𝗮𝗶𝗻𝘀𝘁 𝟱𝟱, 𝗮𝗯𝗼𝘂𝘁 𝟰𝟱% 𝗳𝗲𝘄𝗲𝗿. Whole drug names stayed in one piece for the medical version and shattered in the other.

One honest caveat, because this is easy to oversell. 𝗜𝘁 𝗶𝘀 𝗮 𝗳𝗮𝗶𝗿, 𝗹𝗶𝗸𝗲-𝗳𝗼𝗿-𝗹𝗶𝗸𝗲 𝘁𝗲𝘀𝘁. Give the everyday version a much bigger built-in word list and it mostly catches up. So the lesson is "a specialist can be far more efficient on its home turf," 𝗻𝗼𝘁 "𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝘀𝗽𝗲𝗰𝗶𝗮𝗹𝗶𝘀𝘁 𝗮𝗹𝘄𝗮𝘆𝘀 𝘄𝗶𝗻𝘀."

The tiny AI I used is a see-through demo. Everything it writes is made up and is not medical advice.
